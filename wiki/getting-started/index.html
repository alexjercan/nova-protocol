Your first flight

New to Nova Protocol? This is the shortest path from launch to flying, shooting, and knowing where to look next. If you would rather be walked through it one gesture at a time, jump straight to the tutorial - it teaches every control in order.

Launch and start

The game boots into a main menu. Pick New Game - it drops you into the Shakedown Run, a guided tutorial scenario with a ready-made ship, so there is nothing to build first. (Sandbox is the other door: it opens the ship editor to build and test-fly your own hull.)

The first two minutes

Everything moves under real Newtonian physics - momentum persists and nothing dampens you, so you fly the ship, not a cursor. Distances read in units (u) and speeds in units per second (u/s); see the glossary.

  • Burn. Hold W (or Space) for the main-drive burn and aim with the mouse. You point the hull, then thrust where the nose looks. The velocity sphere beside your ship shows where you are actually going.
  • Lock a target. Hold Ctrl to sweep the radar; the hollow box shows what you are about to lock. Settle on it and hold steady for a moment - a short lock-on dwell has to fill (longer the farther the target is) before the lock latches, and sweeping off before then cancels it. A white crosshair is a travel (nav) lock; raise weapons first (see below) and it lands as a red combat lock instead.
  • GOTO. With a lock, tap G and the autopilot flies you there - it burns over, flips, and coasts to a stop just off the target. Any manual input hands the ship straight back to you.
  • Raise weapons and fire. Hold the right mouse button to raise weapons (combat stance); your reticle goes red and the ship is now "hot". Left mouse fires the turrets and launches torpedoes. A torpedo only launches while you hold a red combat lock.

That is the whole core loop: burn, lock, GOTO, shoot. The Shakedown Run walks you through each beat and completes as you go.

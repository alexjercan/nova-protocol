Reference // sections, scenarios, physics

A field guide to the pieces that make up Nova Protocol. For the keys, see the tutorial.

Ship sections

Ships are modular assemblies of sections. Each section is a child of the ship root with its own mass and health, and contributes one behavior to the whole. Knock a section off and the ship loses that capability.

Hull

Passive structure and armor. Provides hitpoints and the connective backbone the other sections mount to.

Controller

The steering system: a proportional-derivative attitude controller that rotates the ship toward a target heading. Required for any ship a player or AI can fly - no live controller, no control.

Thruster

Produces forward thrust and drives the exhaust plume. Throttle is analog, so thrust authority comes from the thruster's magnitude and how many you fit.

Turret

An articulated mount (base / yaw / pitch / barrel) that aims at a locked target with intercept lead and fires bullet projectiles, bounded by its yaw and pitch limits and fire rate.

Torpedo bay

Fires guided torpedoes that home with proportional-navigation guidance and deal blast (area) damage on detonation.

Scenarios

A scenario places asteroids, gravity wells and ships into a world and wires up objectives. Three scenarios ship today:

  • Asteroid field - the main playable scenario: a scatter of small asteroids, one large gravity rock, and a player ship kitted with hull, thruster, turret and torpedo.
  • Menu ambience - the backdrop behind the main menu: a gravity-well planetoid with an AI ship flying a real ORBIT maneuver around it, framed by a fixed cinematic camera.
  • Sandbox test scenario - the practice space the editor drops you into to fly what you built.

Gravity wells

Large asteroids carry gravity wells that pull opted-in entities - ships and torpedoes - with real inverse-square physics (a = mu / r^2). Close to the surface the pull is clamped to a fixed surface value; it fades smoothly to zero across the outer edge of the sphere of influence, and is zero beyond it.

When wells overlap, an entity tracks the dominant well (the strongest pull) with a bit of hysteresis so it does not flicker at the boundary. This is what the ORBIT autopilot flies around: it parks you in a stable circular orbit of the dominant well.

Factions

A minimal three-tier relation model: player, enemy, and neutral. Player ships are on the player side, AI ships are hostile, and projectiles inherit the allegiance of whoever fired them. Locks are hostile-only, and the reticle tints to the relation - cyan for your own, amber for a hostile.

HUD tiers

Every HUD widget carries a tier, and the grave/tilde key cycles the whole display through three levels: All (full chrome plus instruments), Minimal (flight and combat instruments only) and None (a clean screen for cinematic shots).

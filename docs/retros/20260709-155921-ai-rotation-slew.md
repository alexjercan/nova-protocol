# Retro: AI rotation path onto slew_rotation / hull_turn_rate

- TASK: 20260709-155921
- BRANCH: fix/ai-rotation-slew (squash-merged as 0dea609)
- REVIEW ROUNDS: 1 (APPROVE, 1 MINOR, addressed)

What shipped is in the task's Resolution. Second task of the AI combat arc
(docs/spikes/20260709-225508-ai-combat-behaviors.md).

## What went well

- **The physics test earned its keep by failing.** The first acceptance
  (residual spin ~0) failed at 2.3 rad/s; sampling the spin AXIS (nose,
  alternating sign) identified it in minutes as the already-filed bcs
  roll-damping bug (20260709-125640), not a defect in this change. The
  instrumented-diagnosis habit from the 125640 evidence notes transferred
  directly.
- **Rescoping the test around a known bug instead of weakening it blindly.**
  The final test pins exactly what this task owns (nose converges onto the
  player and holds for a second) and BOUNDS the roll residual with a
  pointer to 125640 to tighten later - a documented boundary, not a
  loosened assert.
- **Reusing the existing harnesses paid.** Command-level tests mirror
  player.rs's command_lag_tests; the physics test reuses
  integrity::test_support. No new test infrastructure.
- **The review MINOR (three copies of the turn-rate derivation) turned into
  flight::ship_turn_rate folding player, autopilot and AI onto one helper -
  the retune can no longer drift them apart.

## What went wrong

- **Over-torqued test rig masked the real regime.** The first physics rig
  copied flight.rs's deliberately over-torqued max_torque 100, which spun
  roll up far past the dampable threshold and made the failure look
  dramatic (2.3 rad/s) before the stock-regime numbers (10 -> 0.23 rad/s)
  clarified it. Root cause: copied a rig constant without reading the
  comment explaining WHY it was 100 there (clamp-ceiling pinning for
  maneuver outcome tests, irrelevant here).

## What to improve next time

- When borrowing a test rig, read its constants' comments before copying;
  a tuning chosen FOR that harness's purpose can invalidate a different
  assertion.
- When a physics assert fails with a steady residual, sample the axis and
  sign pattern before theorizing - it localized this failure immediately.

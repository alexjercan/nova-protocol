How to play

Tutorial // the Shakedown Run and the controls behind it

Nova Protocol is a build-and-fly space shooter. You take a modular ship into a scenario - an asteroid field with gravity wells, salvage, and hostile ships. The flight computer can fly for you through real thrusters, but the moment you touch a control you take over. The fastest way to learn all of that is the built-in tutorial: start a New Game and fly the Shakedown Run.

The game loop

The game boots into a main menu with four options:

  • New Game - drops you into the Shakedown Run, a guided tutorial scenario with a ready-made ship.
  • Sandbox - opens the ship editor so you can build a ship and test-fly it in a practice scenario.
  • Settings - placeholder for now.
  • Exit - quits (hidden in the browser build).

In Sandbox you build a ship from sections, then fly it; pressing F1 takes you back to the editor (Sandbox only). In any scenario, Esc pauses the game and gives you Resume / Back to Main Menu / Exit.

The Shakedown Run, beat by beat

The tutorial teaches one gesture at a time and hands you each verb only when you reach the beat that needs it - so a key that answers with a deny buzz early on just is not unlocked yet. Each beat completes the instant the gesture lands.

Part 1 - Burn and look

  1. Burn to Beacon 1. Hold W to burn, tap X to STOP. There is a gentle speed cap here that lifts once you arrive.
  2. Find Beacon 2. It is off your beam. Hold Alt to free-look around and spot it, then burn over.
  3. Recover 3 supply crates from the debris cluster - fly through each one to collect it.

Part 2 - Lock and let the computer fly

  1. Lock Beacon 3. Your targeting computer comes online. Hold Ctrl on the beacon until the white NAV lock sticks. Radar locking is deliberate now: holding Ctrl sweeps and live-locks whatever your look ray is on.
  2. Press GOTO. With the beacon locked, press G and let the computer fly you there.
  3. On to Beacon 4. A new waypoint appears - lock it and press G again.

Part 3 - Gravity and orbit

  1. Coast. You drift into the planetoid's pull - cut the burn and let gravity carry you.
  2. Press ORBIT. Press O and the ship parks itself into a clean orbit around the well.
  3. Break away. Press Z to cancel the autopilot and burn clear.

Part 4 - First blood

  1. Paint the derelict hulk. Hold the right mouse button to raise your weapons, keep Ctrl on the hulk, and watch the corner viewfinder. Raised weapons make the radar commit to a red combat lock instead of the white nav one.
  2. Open fire. Locked on, fire the turrets with the left mouse button.
  3. Drive off the scavenger. Destroying the hulk draws a scavenger picking through your debris field - put it down.

Finish that and the Shakedown is complete. Tap Ctrl to stand your locks down. The belt is yours.

Flight controls

Thrust is analog on a gamepad trigger and on/off on the keyboard. The autopilot verbs each fly a whole maneuver and then hand control back; any manual input disengages them immediately.

Main thruster burn W / Space  or  RT
GOTO - fly to the current lock G  or  North button
ORBIT - park into orbit around a gravity well O  or  D-Pad Down
STOP - face retrograde and burn to rest X  or  East button
CANCEL autopilot (resume manual) Z  or  West button

Targeting and camera

Locking is deliberate. Hold Ctrl to sweep the radar - it live-locks whatever your look ray is on. Your stance picks the slot: weapons lowered gives a white NAV lock (feeds GOTO); weapons raised (right mouse button) gives a red combat lock (feeds turrets and torpedoes). A lock sticks until the target dies, leaves range, or goes cold. Tap Ctrl to clear locks in stages - combat first, then nav.

Free look Alt (hold)  or  LT
Raise weapons (combat stance) Right mouse button (hold)
Radar lock (hold to sweep) Ctrl (hold)  or  D-Pad Up
Clear lock (staged: combat, then nav) Ctrl (tap)  or  D-Pad Up (tap)
Cycle fine-lock component [ / ]  or  D-Pad Left / Right

Weapons

Fire turrets Left mouse button  or  RT2
Launch torpedo Raise weapons, hold a red combat lock, then fire the torpedo bay

Turrets draw their lead from the combat lock and paint an amber pip on the intercept point; a fine-locked section focuses fire on that component. Torpedoes home on the lock with proportional navigation once armed.

# v0.4.0 - The flight toy learns to fight

The release where the pieces built over three prior updates finally point at
each other and shoot. Torpedoes now chase their target with proportional
navigation, a targeting arc tells you what you are locked onto down to the
section, turrets auto-aim with true intercept lead, and an AI wave with a real
behavior state machine turns the shooting range into a fight. Underneath it all,
a flight-assist overhaul balances thrust through the live center of mass - and
for the first time, the game makes noise.

## An enemy with a state machine

The headline: v0.4.0 ships the first **AI combat wave**, and the enemy is no
longer a stationary target. Each AI ship runs a **behavior state machine** -
Idle, Patrol, Engage, Evade, Retreat - flying autopilot-driven patrol routes,
keeping fire discipline, and prioritizing point defense on inbound torpedoes. It
holds a standoff orbit and strafes, remembers threats and evades them, and
launches its own enveloped torpedoes once it has a solution. The AI helm writes
slewed absolute rotation commands, so it turns like a ship instead of snapping.

## Torpedoes that chase

The torpedo stops being a dumb blast-radius projectile and becomes a **guided
weapon**. It steers toward its lock using **proportional navigation** - the
intercept law real missiles use, turning toward where the target will be rather
than where it is. An angular lock-on aim-assist points the shot on launch, an
arming gate keeps it from detonating in your own lap, and a launch particle
burst plus a blast-radius visual make it read at a glance.

## A targeting arc that knows what you are looking at

A guided weapon needs something to guide toward, so v0.4.0 adds a real
**targeting layer**. Ships inside a 550 m acquisition range are auto-acquired by
their signature, and dwelling your focus on one promotes it to a full lock. From
there a **per-section fine-lock** lets you pick out an individual component of
the enemy hull, with aim-snap and cycling to move between them. Turret fire plugs
straight in: turrets **auto-aim with true intercept lead**, solved in the
shooter's own frame so a moving ship's rounds land, preferring the fine-locked
section, then live structure, then the camera ray.

Holding all of this up is a new HUD **substrate**: screen-projected indicators
that anchor to entities or points, size themselves by apparent size, and clamp
to the screen edge with arrows when a target goes off-screen. It carries turret
lead pips and a locked-target readout for range, closing speed and health.

## Friend, foe, or furniture

A **faction and relation model** - hostile, neutral, or own - gives the combat
sandbox a notion of sides. It drives who gets auto-acquired, whose allegiance a
projectile carries, and how a reticle is tinted. A small system that quietly
makes the whole thing coherent.

## Flying the hull honestly

Underneath the combat sits a **flight-assist overhaul**. There is now an assisted
velocity-hold mode (WASDQE nudges, an X brake latch, a soft speed cap) alongside
a Z direct Newtonian mode for raw thrust, all working against an RCS budget with
a live FA and speed readout. The piece that matters most: the flight computer
**balances thrust through the live center of mass**, using differential throttle
to null the torque a burn would induce and recruiting off-axis thrusters for
counter-torque, so an asymmetric or battle-damaged ship still flies straight.
Handling is now mass-legible - turn rate comes from the torque budget and live
inertia (max torque dropped 100 -> 40), so stripped ships snap and heavy builds
lumber.

The full narrative behind these systems - why proportional navigation, how the
AI decides, and what it took to fly a damaged hull honestly - is in the
[Devlog #4 writeup](../../blog/devlog-4-guided-torpedoes-targeting-and-enemy-ai/).

## Sound and fury

For the first time, the game has **audio**: placeholder SFX for explosions,
impacts, turret fire, torpedo launches, and a throttle-tracking thruster loop,
all with distance attenuation and throttling so a busy fight does not turn into
noise. Alongside it, **combat juice** - trauma-model camera shake and expanding
hit and impact flash rings, also distance-attenuated and throttled. One hit plays
exactly one cue: the audio and juice observers ignore damage-propagation
re-entry, so the feedback stays crisp.

## Under the hood

The integrity, health, blast and mesh-slicer systems now come from the shared
`bevy-common-systems` crate instead of in-tree copies, and the torpedo section
split into its own module. Ships, asteroids and torpedoes interpolate between
physics ticks so the camera stops twitching, and the chase camera anchors on the
live center of mass. Blast damage reaches every body overlapping the blast,
section overkill is absorbed instead of propagated, projectiles no longer collide
with their shooter, and player lock range grew 2 km -> 20 km. A row of example
test ranges (`06_torpedo_range`, `08_turret_range`, `10_gameplay`,
`11_com_range`) ships with live tuning sliders and a headless screenshot smoke
harness, plus a new CI workflow running fmt, clippy and the test suite on every
push. Full, terse detail for every one of these lives in
[CHANGELOG.md](https://github.com/alexjercan/nova-protocol/blob/master/CHANGELOG.md).

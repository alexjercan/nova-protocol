# v0.1.0 - A cube learns to fly

This is where Nova Protocol started. The pitch was to watch a little of _The
Expanse_ and build a game like it, and v0.1.0 is the first playable answer:
build a modular ship out of sections, fly it on real thrusters, and pull other
ships apart with turret fire. It is rough, but the shape of every later system
is already visible here.

## Ships built from sections

A ship in v0.1.0 is not one object but a set of **sections** bolted together.
Thruster sections apply impulse in the direction they face, so binding them to
keys gives you forward drive and - placed to generate torque - rotation. Every
section carries its own health, which means damage is local from day one: shoot
a piece off and the rest of the hull keeps flying.

## Steering that does not fight you

Hand-flying on raw thrusters gets old fast, so a dedicated steering section
reads the mouse delta and turns it into a target rotation, driven by a
proportional-derivative controller that slews the hull smoothly instead of
snapping. This is the seed the flight computer later grew from.

## Turrets and first combat

The turret section sits on the hull with a yaw base and a tilt head so it can
track in two axes and aim where the ship designates. Firing spawns a muzzle
flash and a projectile that flies forward and raycasts each tick to see what it
hit, with a clamped slew rate so the guns track instead of snapping around.

## Satisfying destruction

Hits have to mean something, so damage comes from both turret rounds and ramming
- scaled by collision velocity - and a section that reaches zero health does not
just vanish. A mesh slicer breaks it into chunks that physics flings apart, so a
kill reads like a real explosion rather than a disappearing box.

## Editor and a place to fly

Two scenes wrap it all up: an editor scene for assembling a ship out of
sections, and a simulation scene to drop it into and fly. Together they make
v0.1.0 a complete loop - build a ship, take it out, and blow things (including
yourself) up.

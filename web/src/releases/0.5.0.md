# v0.5.0 - Lock, look, and a front door

The release where automatic targeting becomes a deliberate skill. All the
passive auto-acquisition from v0.4.0 is gone, replaced by hold-to-lock radar, a
corner viewfinder that renders your target through a second camera, and a
kill cam when it dies. New Game now teaches you to fly through the Shakedown Run
tutorial, damage picked up a typed grain, the game boots into a real main menu,
and the whole project got a home on the web. For the narrative version of this
release, read
[Devlog #5](../../blog/devlog-5-radar-locking-shakedown-and-the-web/); this page
is the structured reference.

## Locking is a decision now

Deliberate **radar locking** replaces all passive targeting. Hold CTRL to sweep
and live-lock whatever you look at; your stance picks the slot - lowered NAV
versus raised combat - and a tap clears it. A lock sticks until the target dies,
leaves range, or goes cold. LOCK is framed as a ship-computer capability, the
same way GOTO is.

The lock language got slot-colored to match: a RED bracket is a combat lock,
WHITE is a nav lock, and the old relation tints and reticle pips were retired.
Turrets hold the combat lock even while you aim manually.

## The target viewfinder and kill cam

A **target viewfinder** makes a lock feel like something you have: a corner inset
renders a live, magnified 3D view of your combat lock through a second camera. It
shows a red armed frame while your weapons are hot, a NO-SIGNAL panel for bodies
that cannot be scoped, and a roughly two-second freeze-frame **kill cam** when
the target dies. The fine-locked section glows in both the main view and the
inset.

The inset zooms combat bodies only - ships, torpedoes, asteroids - not beacons,
and frames section-less bodies by their collider bounds.

## The Shakedown Run

New Game now starts the **Shakedown Run**, a roughly twelve-beat tutorial that
walks a new pilot through the whole vocabulary: burn, freelook, stop, salvage,
GOTO, coasting through a gravity well, ORBIT, radar lock, a live-fire rehearsal
on a derelict, and a real scavenger fight. Each beat teaches exactly one gesture
and completes the instant that gesture lands, so it never blocks a player who
already knows the moves.

Making it playable shook out a long tail of fixes - park points sized to their
beacon triggers, orbit-hold completion, scavenger spawn timing and a 150u combat
leash, invulnerable planetoids, speed-cap teaching, and readable objective
pacing. Asteroid kills now emit OnDestroyed under the scenario id (which had
soft-locked the derelict beat).

## Damage with a grain

Damage is now **typed** - Kinetic, AP, EMP, Explosive - and checked against
per-section resistance tables, so what you shoot matters as much as where you
shoot it. Each turret carries a loaded-ammo slot that sets its rounds' type, with
a color-coded ammo readout.

The point-defense cannon was retuned to actually be point defense: per-hit damage
dropped from 20 to 4, so the stream chips a target down over a visible burst
instead of deleting it in one frame. Turret bullets are now sensor projectiles -
same damage, no physical shove, expended on first contact - and their rounds
curve through gravity wells like ships and torpedoes.

## A game that boots into a menu

v0.5.0 boots into a **main menu** with a live ambient backdrop, an AI ship flying
a thruster-driven orbit, plus an ESC pause menu, all living in a new `nova_menu`
crate. The HUD learned **visibility levels** - grave/tilde cycles ALL, MINIMAL,
NONE - and the flight readouts went diegetic: speed and engaged-mode chips beside
the velocity sphere, an ORBIT radius spoke, and a nav-cyan tint while the
autopilot flies.

Objectives got easier to follow, with a gold marker chip carrying live distance
to the current target, glowing salvage crates, contextual keybind rows that
appear only when their verb can act (emphasized keys pulse gold), and a
completion chime. Underneath, the scenario system gained new primitives - nav
beacons, salvage crates with authorable radar signatures, despawn-by-id, and
events like OnOrbit, OnTravelLock and OnCombatLock - which is what made the
Shakedown Run expressible as data. The editor also learned to show a section's
bound key as a chip and rebind it on click.

## A home on the web

v0.5.0 ships a **web landing site** (the `web/` directory: TypeScript, Webpack
and Tailwind), and the GitHub Pages deploy now serves it at the root with the
playable game tucked under `/play/`. The devlogs and the game's new web home
live here now (the wiki and tutorial follow in v0.5.2).

## Under the hood

The debug inspector stays on a window camera instead of leaking into
render-to-texture cameras, HUD apparent-size elements measure real colliders
rather than trigger volumes, and F1 back-to-editor is sandbox-only. Full, terse
detail for every one of these lives in
[CHANGELOG.md](https://github.com/alexjercan/nova-protocol/blob/master/CHANGELOG.md).

Wiki / Ship sections

Ship sections

A ship in Nova Protocol is not a monolithic model - it is a root entity with a handful of section children. Each section is mounted to the hull, carries its own mass and health, and contributes exactly one behavior to the whole ship. Knock a section off and the ship loses that capability but keeps flying on whatever is left.

Hull

Passive structure and armor. It provides hitpoints and the connective backbone the other sections mount to. A hull has no active behavior of its own - it is what the rest of the ship is built around.

Controller

The steering system: a proportional-derivative attitude controller that rotates the ship toward a target heading. It is required for any ship a player or AI can fly - with no live controller, the hull cannot steer itself, so destroying it disables a ship without destroying it outright.

Thruster

Produces forward thrust and drives the exhaust plume. Throttle is analog, so a build's thrust authority comes from each thruster's magnitude and how many you fit. The flight computer balances thrust through the live center of mass, so off-axis or battle-damaged thruster layouts still fly straight (see Flight & autopilot).

Turret

An articulated mount (base / yaw / pitch / barrel) that aims at a locked target with true intercept lead and fires bullet projectiles, bounded by its yaw and pitch limits and its fire rate. Turrets draw their aim from the combat lock - see Combat & weapons.

Torpedo bay

Fires guided torpedoes that home with proportional-navigation guidance and deal blast (area-of-effect) damage on detonation. A torpedo is a section like any other, so it inherits the same physics and mounting rules as the rest of the build.

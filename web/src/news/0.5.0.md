# v0.5.0 - Lock, look, and a front door

v0.4.0 gave Nova Protocol combat, an AI to fight, and a lot of automatic
targeting. v0.5.0 takes that targeting and hands the controls back to the player,
deliberately: passive auto-acquisition is gone, replaced by hold-to-lock radar, a
corner viewfinder that renders your target through a second camera, and a kill cam
when it dies. It also does the thing every project eventually has to do - it teaches
a new player how to fly through the Shakedown Run, gives damage a typed grain, boots
into a real main menu, and puts up a front door on the web. Here is the whole of it.

## Locking is a decision now

The biggest change in feel: all the passive auto-targeting from the last release is
gone, replaced by deliberate **radar locking**. Hold CTRL to sweep and live-lock
whatever you look at; your stance picks the slot - lowered gives a white NAV
crosshair that feeds GOTO, raised gives a red combat reticle that feeds guns,
torpedoes and fine-lock. Tapping CTRL clears the lock in stages - combat first, then
nav - and a lock sticks until the target dies, leaves range, or goes cold.
Crucially, LOCK is now framed as a ship-computer capability, the same way GOTO is:
it is something the ship does for you, not a magic HUD overlay.

The lock language got slot-colored to match: a red bracket is a combat lock, white
is a nav lock, and the old relation tints and reticle pips were retired. Turrets
hold the combat lock even while you aim manually.

## The target viewfinder and kill cam

To make a lock feel like something you have, v0.5.0 adds a **target viewfinder**: a
corner inset that renders a live, magnified 3D view of your combat lock through a
second camera. It shows a red armed frame while your weapons are hot, a NO-SIGNAL
panel for bodies that cannot be scoped, and - the favorite touch - a roughly
two-second freeze-frame **kill cam** when the target dies. The fine-locked section
glows in both the main view and the inset, so you always know exactly what you are
shooting at.

The inset zooms combat bodies only - ships, torpedoes, asteroids - not beacons, and
frames section-less bodies by their collider bounds.

## The Shakedown Run

New Game now starts the **Shakedown Run**, a roughly twelve-beat tutorial that walks
a new pilot through the whole vocabulary: burn, freelook, stop, salvage, GOTO,
coasting through a gravity well, ORBIT, radar lock, a live-fire rehearsal on a
derelict, and finally a real scavenger fight. Each beat teaches exactly one gesture
and completes the instant that gesture lands, so it never blocks a player who already
knows the moves.

Making it playable shook out a long tail of playtest fixes - park points sized to
their beacon triggers, orbit-hold completion, scavenger spawn timing and a 150u
combat leash, invulnerable planetoids, speed-cap teaching, and readable objective
pacing. Asteroid kills now emit OnDestroyed under the scenario id, which had
soft-locked the derelict beat.

## Damage with a grain

Combat picked up some texture too. Damage is now **typed** - Kinetic, AP, EMP,
Explosive - and checked against per-section resistance tables, so what you shoot
matters as much as where you shoot it. Each turret carries a loaded-ammo slot that
sets its rounds' type, with a color-coded ammo readout.

The point-defense cannon was retuned to actually be point defense: per-hit damage
dropped from 20 to 4, so the stream chips a target down over a visible burst instead
of deleting it in one frame. Turret bullets are now sensor projectiles - same damage,
no physical shove, expended on first contact - and their rounds curve through gravity
wells like ships and torpedoes.

## A game that boots into a menu

v0.5.0 is the release where Nova Protocol starts to feel like a shipped game rather
than a scene loader. It boots into a **main menu** with a live ambient backdrop - an
AI ship flying a thruster-driven orbit - plus an ESC pause menu, all living in a new
`nova_menu` crate. The HUD learned **visibility levels** - grave/tilde cycles ALL,
MINIMAL, NONE - and the flight readouts went diegetic: speed and engaged-mode chips
sit beside the velocity sphere, an ORBIT radius spoke, and a nav-cyan tint while the
autopilot flies.

Objectives got easier to follow, with a gold marker chip carrying live distance to
the current target, glowing salvage crates, contextual keybind rows that appear only
when their verb can act (emphasized keys pulse gold), and a completion chime.
Underneath, the scenario system gained new primitives - nav beacons, salvage crates
with authorable radar signatures, despawn-by-id, and events like OnOrbit,
OnTravelLock and OnCombatLock - which is what made the Shakedown Run expressible as
data. The editor also learned to show a section's bound key as a chip and rebind it
on click.

## A home on the web

Finally, the thing you are reading this on. v0.5.0 ships a **web landing site** (the
`web/` directory: TypeScript, Webpack and Tailwind), and the GitHub Pages deploy now
serves it at the root with the playable game tucked under `/play/`. This news feed
and the game's new web home start here, with the wiki and tutorial following shortly
after in v0.5.2.

## Under the hood

The debug inspector stays on a window camera instead of leaking into
render-to-texture cameras, HUD apparent-size elements measure real colliders rather
than trigger volumes, and F1 back-to-editor is sandbox-only.

## Point releases

Shortly after 0.5.0 shipped, **v0.5.1** cleaned up two crashes unique to the shipped
WASM build - both only bit the browser when you started a run (New Game from the menu
or Play from the editor) and never touched native. The target inset was carrying a
`view_formats` override that WebGL2 does not support for a plain sRGB target, so it
now drops that override. And the skybox cubemap's `.meta` loader settings actually
apply in the shipped app again: `AssetMetaCheck::Never` had silently ignored them,
which resurrected an oversized-upload race that crashes GPUs with a 16384 texture
limit.

A little later, **v0.5.2** turned the site into a place you can actually learn from.
A full **wiki** landed - gameplay, ship sections, keybinds, world, and meta pages -
alongside a tutorial trimmed to first-scenario onboarding. Gamepad bindings got
rounded out (ORBIT to South, scenario-advance confirm to DPadDown, plus buttons for
HUD cycle, pause, and back-to-editor), and the examples became a testable curriculum:
twelve numbered examples self-driving under `BCS_AUTOPILOT` with behavior assertions,
gating every push now that CI's example smoke suite is BLOCKING again. A handful of
audio and teardown bugs got fixed too - thruster hum now attenuates with distance per
ship, and scenario teardown no longer logs an "Entity despawned" command error when a
ship despawns with autopilot engaged.
